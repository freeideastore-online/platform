# Brainstorming Log

- The need is real, but trust is the product.
- A marketplace may be too risky as the first version.
- A safer starting point could be information, comparison, checklists, and school-approved options.
- The first useful artifact may be a suburb-by-suburb guide, not an app.
- Schools may be a better distribution channel than direct parent acquisition.
- Families often need supervision, not just transport, especially for younger children.

Potential product shapes:

- parent-facing directory of verified options by school/suburb;
- school transport comparison page;
- recurring ride coordination checklist;
- handover and child-seat compliance checklist;
- concierge service that helps families set up a recurring arrangement;
- future marketplace only if safety, insurance, and compliance are solved.

The idea should stay conservative because the users are young children.
