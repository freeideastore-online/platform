# Problem And Customer

The first customer is a parent of a primary-school child who needs after-school pickup help, bus alternatives, or shared transport coordination.

Schools, councils, and existing transport providers are important stakeholders.

For ages 5 to 10, the real problem is usually:

- work starts before school drop-off;
- work finishes after school pickup;
- siblings have conflicting activities;
- a parent cannot reliably handle a recurring route;
- the child needs an adult to hand them over safely;
- the family does not know which services are legitimate nearby.

The customer may be a parent, but the buyer or trusted introducer may be a school, after-school program, local council, or childcare-adjacent provider.

The product must answer "Can I trust this arrangement with my child?" before it answers "How much does it cost?"
