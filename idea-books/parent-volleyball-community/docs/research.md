# Research Notes

Best existing apps to try:

- BAND is the best immediate choice for a casual parent community. It supports private groups, posts, albums, calendar events, RSVP-style coordination, reminders, and birthdays or special occasions.
- Spond is best if the group becomes more sport-focused. It is stronger for recurring sports sessions, RSVP tracking, attendance history, payments, roles, and parent or guardian workflows.
- Stack Team App is a good structured-club option with events, RSVP, attendance tracking, galleries, access groups, and parent/child member handling.
- Klubraum is a privacy-first club/community option with calendar, chat, attendance, and member management.
- TeamSnap is strongest for formal youth sports teams, but may feel too sports-season oriented.
- InstaTeam covers schedules, reminders, messaging, photos, videos, attendance reports, and parent/player coordination.
- sportsYou fits school or athletics-style communication better than informal parent friendship groups.
- TeamReach is simple and free, but likely too light for birthdays and member memory.
- GroupMe is better than WhatsApp for event albums, but still weak for profiles, birthdays, and attendance history.

Why WhatsApp is not enough:

- birthdays are not structured group data;
- photos disappear into chat history;
- albums are not organized around events or families;
- attendance history is not tracked;
- repeated participation cannot easily be recognized;
- new members cannot quickly understand who is who;
- child photo consent is hard to manage.

No reviewed app clearly automates "came three Saturdays in a row" appreciation for this exact use case.
