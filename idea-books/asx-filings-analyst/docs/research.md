# Research Notes

- Public sources include ASX announcements, company annual reports, quarterly activity reports, investor presentations, and governance disclosures.
- Inputs should be traceable and timestamped.
- The app must separate factual extraction from interpretation.
- Market data licensing, delayed pricing, and financial advice rules are the main risk areas.

Existing Australian tools already cover parts of this market. A plain undervalued-stock screener is not novel.

Competitors and substitutes include:

- Simply Wall St for ASX screeners, fair value estimates, company reports, and portfolio tooling.
- Morningstar Australia for stock research, fair value analysis, ratings, and undervalued stock lists.
- Market Index for ASX screeners, valuation filters, scans, and company data.
- Stock Doctor for ASX research, financial health ratings, quantitative analysis, and portfolio tooling.
- Intelligent Investor for ASX research, recommendations, weekly reviews, and buy or hold views.
- Investing.com AU for broad screener filters across valuation, dividends, growth, risk, and technicals.

The gap is not screening alone. The likely gap is trustworthy explanation, source tracing, and workflow.

Differentiation should focus on:

- reading ASX announcements and company reports;
- extracting financial facts from source documents;
- linking every important claim to a public source;
- explaining why a company appeared on the watchlist;
- showing the counterargument and risk flags;
- tracking what changed since the last report;
- helping the user save, dismiss, tag, and annotate companies.

Main risks:

- competitors already cover generic screens;
- market-data redistribution can become expensive or restricted;
- trust is hard because financial errors are costly;
- the product can drift into regulated financial advice;
- users may not pay unless the output saves real time;
- AI hallucination is unacceptable unless claims are source-backed.
