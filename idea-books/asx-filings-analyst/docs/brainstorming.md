# Brainstorming Log

- The original question was whether an app could analyze public Australian market information and suggest undervalued stocks each week.
- The strongest version is not a magic stock picker. It is a disciplined evidence collector and explanation layer.
- A good weekly output should explain the valuation signal, the business quality signal, the risk signal, and the exact public source used.
- The weakest version is another stock screener that says which stocks are cheap.
- The defensible version reads filings, identifies changes, links evidence, shows the counterargument, and helps the user keep a research notebook.

The app should avoid the phrase "weekly buy recommendations". It should use language like "research watchlist", "companies to investigate", "source-backed valuation signals", and "possible value trap".

The product is useful if it saves a careful investor time without asking them to trust a black box.
